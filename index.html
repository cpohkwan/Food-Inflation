From Lunchflation Woes to Global Food Inflation Pains

As I sob over the 25-cent hike in my daily caffeine fix, I realise people in some parts of the world are grappling with triple-digit hyperinflation.

A story by Cheong Poh Kwan

For years, I could grab this cup of coffee at my school’s canteen for S$0.55 (US$ 0.39). Bringing my own mug will save me another 10 cents.

That changed a few weeks ago when the price was jacked up to $0.70. No more discount for eco-conscious efforts either.

While facing lunchflation as a salaried staff, I also find myself having to raise the selling price of my homemade soup paste from S$8.8, my favorite number, to $9.50, my husband’s favorite number. But the marginal increase is hardly enough to cover the 20% hike in our packaging cost and another 20% hike in our delivery cost thanks to soaring oil and gas prices.
